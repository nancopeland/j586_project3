Millennials: Not the Worst Generation

Millennials get a bad rep. Supposedly, they're narcissists who feel the need to photographically document their lives via Snapchat and butcher the English language with overused acronyms. While these assumptions aren't wrong, Millennials have more going for them than negative stereotypes. The Millennial generation is significantly more diverse and forward-thinking than past generations and the U.S. as a whole.

By Nan Copeland

Who Exactly
are Millennials?

Millennials are the generation born between 1980 and 2000. They are called "Millennials" because they grew up around the turn of the century. The term was first coined by Neil Howe and William Strauss in their book, "Millennials Rising: The Next Great Generation." They make up 28.7% of the U.S. population, making them bigger than the Baby Boomers, who make up 23.7% of the population.

Diversity

Millennials are more diverse than past generations. Overall, Millennials have 3% less white people and 4% more Hispanic people than the total U.S. population. In the past 50 years, the influx of Latinos from Mexico has contributed to this change. The only generation with more Hispanics than the Millennial generation is iGeneration, the generation born after 2000. It is predicted that the U.S. will become a majority-minority population by 2043. The diversity among Millennials has resulted in a more progressive and liberal population than past generations.

Political Views

Millennials don't really care about politics and, as you can see from the chart, can't decide where they fall on the political spectrum. But, in general, Millennials are significantly more liberal than past generations. Only 15% of Millennials define themselves as conservative and even the ones who do are more likely to support liberal issues, such as homosexuality, immigration and the environment, than other generations.
